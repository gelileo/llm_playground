Les Baum - Class of 1946






Standout Wrestler at Hershey High School
Won 4 Varsity Letters in Wrestling
Wrestled at 103 lbs his Junior year and 112 lbs his Senior year
2nd place District Ill in Wrestling his Senior year
2nd place PIAA State Finals in Wrestling his Senior year
All Navy-Marine Wrestling Champion at 114.5 lbs in 1948
Finished 4th place in A.A.U. Olympic tryouts at 114.5 lbs in 1952
Earned the NCAA Award given for coaching Wrestling for 25 years
Inducted into the Ohio Wrestling Hall of Fame in 1981
Inducted into the Findlay College Sports Hall of Fame
Inducted into the Ohio High School Wrestling Coaches Association Hall of Fame in 1981
Coached Wrestling for over 20 years at Urbana University
Inducted into the Urbana University Hall of Fame in 1995
Inducted into the Ohio Wrestling Officials Hall of Fame
Won 3 Varsity Letters in Wrestling at 114.5lb weight class at Findlay College
Won 2 Varsity Letters in Track and Field at Findlay College
Head Wrestling Coach:
	
Muskingum College
Elizabethtown College
Kenyon College


Assistant Wrestling Coach:
	
Findlay College
The Ohio State University (Freshman Coach)
Cross Country Coach:
Elizabethtown College
Urbana University


Athletic Director:
	
Elizabethtown College
Urbana University









Michael Horrocks - Class of 1981






Won 2 Varsity Letters in Football at Hershey
Team Co-Captain his Senior Year in Football
Starting Quarterback and Safety his Senior year 
Named to the Capital Area Conference 2nd Team on Defense his Senior year in Football
Won 2 Varsity Letters in Baseball at Hershey
Member of the Capital Area Conference East Division Champions in Baseball his Junior Year at Hershey
Won 2 Varsity Letters in Football at West Chester University
2-year starter at Quarterback at West Chester Universitv
Led the Team to a 2-year record of 14 - 7 as starting Quarterback at West Chester University
Led the team to a win over Div. 1AA University of Delaware in his first start at Quarterback his Junior year. That was the first time West Chester had beaten Delaware in 23 years.
Set a School Single game passing record with 377 yards against Shippensburg his Junior year in Football at West Chester University
Was the top Rushing Quarterback in the Pennsylvania State Athletic Conference his Junior year in Football, running for 373 yards.
Was named to the PSAC All East 2nd Team in Offense his Junior and Senior years at West Chester University
Named PSAC East Division Offensive Player of the Week his Senior year for his play against Millersville where he ran for 2 Touchdowns and passed for 234 yards and 2 Touchdowns
Inducted into the West Chester University Athletic Hall of Fame
Inducted into the Capital Area Chapter of the Pennsylvania Sports Hall of Fame (2018)
Veteran of the United States Marine Corps; served in the Persian Gulf War (1990-1991)
Co-Pilot of United Flight 175, which crashed into the South Tower of the World Trade Center in New York City on September 11, 2001







Scott Pera - Class of 1985






Won 2 Varsity letters in Basketball at Hershey
Mid Penn Conference Champions in Basketball his Junior and Senior year
Set the record for assists in a single season in Basketball his Senior year
Held the record for career assists in Basketball at Hershey
Won 2 Varsity Letters in Baseball at Hershey
2-time first team Mid Penn Conference All Star at 2nd Base
2-time Team Spirit Award winner in Baseball
District III Runner up in Baseball his Senior year
Coaching Career
	
1992-93 Assistant Basketball Coach at Elizabethtown College
1993-95 Assistant Basketball Coach at Palmyra High School
1995-2000 Head Basketball Coach at Annville Cleona High School
1999 Coached the Team to the PIAA AA State Championship
2000-2006 Head Basketball Coach at Artesia High School in Lakewood, CA
2006 Coached the team to the State Championship
2006-2012 Director of Basketball Operations/Assistant Coach at Arizona State University
2012-2014 Assistant Coach at University of Pennsylvania
2014-2017 Assistant Coach at Rice University
2017-Present Head Coach Rice University
Has won over 300 games as a Head Coach in High School and College









Bruce Shenk - Class of 1954






Won 2 Varsity letters in Football at Hershey
Member of the 1953 South Penn Champions in Football
Member of the 1954 South Penn Champions that were undefeated and unscored upon in League play
Won 2 Varsity letters in Basketball at Hershey
Won 3 Varsity letters in Track and Field at Hershey
Undefeated in dual meets in the 440 and 880 in Track and Field his Junior and Senior year at Hershey
2nd place in the South Penn in the 880 his Junior year
Anchored 2 winning relay teams (Mile and Medley) at the Lebanon Relays his Senior year
1st place in the 880 and Mile relay (anchor) in the South Penn Championships his Senior year
District III Runner up in the 880 his Senior year
5th place in the PIAA State Championships in the 880 his senior year
Won 3 Varsity letters in Football at West Chester University
2nd team All PA State College Football Team his Sophomore year
1st team All PA State College Football Team his Junior and Senior year
Tied for 1st in the NCAA National Small College Touchdown Pass receiving his Junior and Senior years
Named a 2-time Small College All American
Maxwell Club Small College Player of the Year
Won the 880 and the Mile relay (anchor) in the State College
Championships his Sophomore year
Won the 440 and 880 in the State College Championships his junior year
Won the 880 in the Middle Atlantic Championships his senior year
1st place in the Mile Relay (anchor) at the Penn Relays his senior year
2-time Team Captain in Track and Field at West Chester University
Inducted into the West Chester University Hall of Fame







Charles "Charlie" Trayer - Class of 1972






Won 3 Varsity letters in Cross Country at Hershey
Team Captain his Senior year in Cross Country
Finished 1st in 6 meets his Senior year
Finished 2nd in 3 meets his Senior vear
Set School records for the Hershey course as well as three other conference schools' courses
2-time District Ill qualifier in Cross Country
Won 2 Varsity letters in Track and Field at Hershey
Won the 2-mile event at 9 of 10 Regular season meets his Junior year
Set the School record in the 2 mile his Junior year, only to later that season break his own record
Undefeated in the 2 mile his Senior year and broke the Hershey record 3 more times that season
Set the 2-mile record at 2 other Conference schools
2-time District Ill qualifier in Track and Field
Presented the Hershey Lions Club Outstanding Athlete Award his Senior year
Won 8 Varsity letters in Cross Country and Track and Field at Millersville University
Was the most consistent points winner for the Millersville Cross Country team
4-year qualifier for State and Conference meets at Millersville University
3-time qualifier for the National meet at Millersville University
Competed in numerous marathons, including Boston, New York, Baltimore and New Orleans
Qualified for 1980 Olympic Trials as a marathon runner
Coached the Oley Valley High School Cross Country team
Named the "Ultrarunner of the Year" by Ultra Running Magazine in 1987
Recipient of the Alumni Cross Country Award at Millersville in 1976
